# Coffee Brewing Tips

A comprehensive guide to brewing the perfect cup of coffee at home.

## Brewing Methods

### Pour Over (V60 / Chemex)

**Grind Size:** Medium-fine (like coarse sand)
**Ratio:** 1:16 (1g coffee to 16g water)
**Temperature:** 195-205°F (90-96°C)

**Steps:**

1. Rinse filter with hot water to remove paper taste
2. Add ground coffee, zero the scale
3. Bloom: Pour 2x coffee weight in water, wait 30-45 seconds
4. Pour in slow circles, avoiding the edges
5. Total brew time: 2.5-3.5 minutes

**Tips:**

- Use a gooseneck kettle for precise control
- Pour slowly and steadily
- The bloom should puff up like a dome

### French Press

**Grind Size:** Coarse (like sea salt)
**Ratio:** 1:15 (1g coffee to 15g water)
**Temperature:** 200°F (93°C)

**Steps:**

1. Add coarse ground coffee to press
2. Pour hot water, saturating all grounds
3. Stir gently, then place lid without pressing
4. Steep for 4 minutes
5. Press down slowly and steadily
6. Serve immediately or transfer to another vessel

**Tips:**

- Don't let it sit after pressing (over-extraction)
- Plunge slowly to avoid sediment
- Preheat the press with hot water

### AeroPress

**Grind Size:** Medium-fine
**Ratio:** 1:16
**Temperature:** 175-185°F (80-85°C)

**Steps:**

1. Insert plunger slightly into chamber
2. Add coffee, then water up to the number
3. Stir for 10 seconds
4. Attach filter cap with rinsed paper
5. Flip onto mug after 1-2 minutes
6. Press gently for 20-30 seconds

**Tips:**

- The inverted method is popular for full immersion
- Experiment with brew times
- Metal filters give more body, paper gives cleaner taste

### Espresso

**Grind Size:** Fine (like table salt)
**Ratio:** 1:2 (18g in, 36g out typical)
**Temperature:** 195-205°F (90-96°C)
**Pressure:** 9 bars

**Steps:**

1. Grind fresh, dose into portafilter
2. Distribute grounds evenly
3. Tamp level with 30 lbs pressure
4. Lock in and start extraction immediately
5. Target 25-30 seconds for 36g output

**Tips:**

- Fresh beans are crucial (2-4 weeks post-roast)
- Consistent tamping pressure matters
- Time from grind to extraction should be minimal

## Coffee Storage

**Do:**

- Store in airtight container
- Keep in cool, dark place
- Buy whole beans, grind before brewing
- Use within 2-4 weeks of roast date

**Don't:**

- Store in freezer (causes condensation)
- Store in clear containers in light
- Buy pre-ground coffee if possible
- Keep near heat sources

## Water Quality

Coffee is 98% water. Use filtered water for best results.

**Ideal water parameters:**

- TDS: 75-250 ppm
- pH: 6.5-7.5
- No chlorine or strong odors

## Common Problems

| Problem    | Cause                       | Solution                                 |
| ---------- | --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Bitter     | Over-extracted              | Use coarser grind or shorter time        |
| Sour       | Under-extracted             | Use finer grind or longer time           |
| Weak       | Not enough coffee           | Increase coffee amount or decrease water |
| Too strong | Too much coffee             | Decrease coffee or increase water        |
| Muddy      | Grind too fine / old coffee | Use coarser grind or fresher beans       |

## Equipment Maintenance

- Clean your grinder weekly (use grinder cleaning pellets)
- Descale espresso machines every 2-3 months
- Replace water filters as recommended
- Clean pour-over vessels after each use

| AeroPress    | 15g    | 240g  | ~220ml |

| Cold Brew    | 100g   | 700g  | ~600ml |
