# Health Goals for 2026

My personal health and wellness roadmap for the year 2026. Documented for accountability and progress tracking.

## Overview

This year focuses on building sustainable habits around fitness, nutrition, sleep, and mental wellness. The goal is consistency over intensity.

## Fitness Goals

### Cardiovascular Health

- **Target:** 150 minutes of moderate cardio per week
- **Activities:**
  - Running (3x per week, 30-45 minutes)
  - Swimming (1x per week)
  - Cycling (weekend long rides)
- **Milestones:**
  - Q1: Build base to run 5k comfortably
  - Q2: Complete first 10k race
  - Q3: Train for half marathon
  - Q4: Run half marathon under 2 hours

### Strength Training

- **Target:** 3 sessions per week
- **Focus Areas:**
  - Core stability and posture
  - Lower body strength (squats, deadlifts)
  - Upper body functional strength
- **Progression Goals:**
  - Bodyweight squat: 3 sets of 20
  - Push-ups: 3 sets of 15
  - Plank hold: 3 minutes
  - Deadlift: 1.5x bodyweight

### Flexibility & Recovery

- Daily 10-minute stretching routine
- Yoga 2x per week
- Monthly massage or foam rolling session
- Rest days are sacred - minimum 1 per week

## Nutrition Goals

### Daily Targets

- **Calories:** 2,200-2,400 (maintenance with slight deficit)
- **Protein:** 140g minimum (0.7g per lb bodyweight)
- **Water:** 3 liters minimum
- **Fiber:** 30g minimum

### Eating Habits

- **Meal Prep:** Sunday batch cooking for weekdays
- **Protein Priority:** Include protein with every meal
- **Vegetable Focus:** Half plate vegetables at lunch and dinner
- **Mindful Eating:** No screens during meals, chew thoroughly

### Foods to Emphasize

- Leafy greens (spinach, kale, arugula)
- Lean proteins (chicken, fish, tofu, legumes)
- Whole grains (quinoa, brown rice, oats)
- Nuts and seeds (almonds, chia, flax)
- Berries and colorful fruits
- Fermented foods (yogurt, kimchi, sauerkraut)

### Foods to Limit

- Processed sugars (keep under 25g/day)
- Refined carbohydrates
- Alcohol (max 2 drinks/week)
- Ultra-processed foods
- Late-night eating (nothing after 8 PM)

### Meal Timing

- Breakfast: 7:00-8:00 AM
- Lunch: 12:00-1:00 PM
- Snack: 3:00-4:00 PM (if needed)
- Dinner: 6:00-7:00 PM
- Fasting window: 8 PM - 7 AM (11 hours)

## Sleep Goals

### Sleep Hygiene

- **Target:** 7.5-8 hours per night
- **Bedtime:** 10:30 PM
- **Wake time:** 6:30 AM

### Sleep Routine

- 9:30 PM: Begin wind-down (no screens)
- 9:45 PM: Light reading or meditation
- 10:15 PM: Prepare for bed
- 10:30 PM: Lights out

### Sleep Environment

- Keep bedroom cool (65-68°F)
- Blackout curtains or eye mask
- White noise machine
- No phones in bedroom (use analog alarm)

## Mental Wellness Goals

### Daily Practices

- Morning meditation: 10 minutes
- Gratitude journaling: 3 things daily
- Nature exposure: Minimum 20 minutes outside
- Digital sunset: No screens 1 hour before bed

### Weekly Practices

- Social connection: One meaningful conversation
- Creative activity: Drawing, writing, or music
- Learning: Read or listen to something new
- Reflection: Weekly review of goals and progress

### Monthly Practices

- Digital detox: One full day offline
- Nature immersion: Hike or outdoor adventure
- Social event: Connect with friends/family
- Self-care: Massage, spa day, or treat yourself

### Stress Management

- Identify triggers and create coping strategies
- Practice saying "no" to protect energy
- Regular check-ins with therapist (quarterly minimum)
- Breathing exercises for acute stress moments

## Health Metrics to Track

### Monthly Measurements

- Weight (same time, same conditions)
- Waist circumference
- Resting heart rate
- Blood pressure

### Quarterly Assessments

- Blood panel (lipids, glucose, vitamin D)
- Fitness benchmarks (run time, strength tests)
- Progress photos
- Goal review and adjustment

### Annual Checkups

- Comprehensive physical exam
- Dental cleaning (2x per year)
- Eye exam
- Skin check

## Supplements

**Daily:**

- Vitamin D3 (2000 IU)
- Omega-3 fish oil
- Magnesium glycinate (evening)

**As Needed:**

- Vitamin C (when feeling run down)
- Zinc (during cold season)
- Electrolytes (after intense workouts)

## Motivation & Accountability

### Why These Goals Matter

1. **Longevity:** Want to stay active and independent into old age
2. **Energy:** Need sustained energy for work and relationships
3. **Mental clarity:** Exercise and sleep improve focus and creativity
4. **Confidence:** Feeling strong and capable
5. **Role model:** Want to model healthy habits for others

### Accountability Strategies

- Weekly check-in with accountability partner
- Monthly progress review on this document
- Quarterly fitness assessments
- Celebrate small wins along the way

### Reward System

- Q1 goal met: New running shoes
- Q2 goal met: Weekend getaway
- Q3 goal met: Fitness tracker upgrade
- Q4 goal met: Reward TBD based on overall progress

## Emergency Protocols

### When Sick

- Prioritize rest over workouts
- Stay hydrated
- Light movement only if energy allows
- Resume gradually when recovered

### When Injured

- Never push through sharp pain
- Modify workouts to avoid aggravation
- Seek professional help if not improving in 1 week
- Focus on what you CAN do

### When Motivation Fades

- Remember your "why"
- Reduce scope but maintain consistency
- Connect with support system
- Review progress photos and wins
