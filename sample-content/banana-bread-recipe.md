# Banana Bread Recipe

The perfect moist banana bread with a tender crumb and rich banana flavor. This is my go-to recipe that never fails.

## Ingredients

### Wet Ingredients

- 3 large ripe bananas (about 1.5 cups mashed)
- 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
- 2 large eggs, room temperature
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1/4 cup plain Greek yogurt or sour cream

### Dry Ingredients

- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional)

### Sweeteners

- 3/4 cup brown sugar, packed
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar

### Optional Add-ins

- 1/2 cup walnuts or pecans, chopped
- 1/2 cup chocolate chips
- 1/2 cup dried cranberries or raisins
- Streusel topping (see below)

## Equipment

- 9x5 inch loaf pan
- Two mixing bowls
- Whisk or fork
- Rubber spatula
- Wire cooling rack
- Parchment paper (optional but recommended)

## Instructions

### Preparation

1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C)
2. Grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan, or line with parchment paper
3. Let eggs come to room temperature (about 30 minutes on counter)

### Making the Batter

1. **Mash the bananas**
   - Peel and mash 3 ripe bananas in a large bowl
   - Leave some small chunks for texture
   - Should yield about 1.5 cups mashed

2. **Mix wet ingredients**
   - Add melted butter to mashed bananas and stir
   - Whisk in eggs, one at a time
   - Add vanilla extract and Greek yogurt
   - Mix until well combined

3. **Combine sugars**
   - Add brown sugar and granulated sugar
   - Stir until sugars are mostly dissolved
   - Mixture should be slightly thick

4. **Prepare dry ingredients**
   - In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg
   - This ensures even distribution of leavening

5. **Combine wet and dry**
   - Add dry ingredients to wet ingredients
   - Stir with rubber spatula until JUST combined
   - Do not overmix - some lumps are okay
   - Overmixing creates tough, dense bread

6. **Fold in add-ins**
   - Gently fold in nuts, chocolate chips, or other mix-ins
   - Reserve some for topping if desired

### Baking

1. **Transfer to pan**
   - Pour batter into prepared loaf pan
   - Smooth the top with spatula
   - Tap pan gently on counter to remove air bubbles

2. **Optional toppings**
   - Sprinkle reserved nuts or chocolate chips on top
   - Add streusel topping (recipe below)
   - Slice a banana lengthwise and press on top

3. **Bake**
   - Bake at 350°F for 55-65 minutes
   - Bread is done when:
     - Top is golden brown
     - Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs
     - Internal temperature reaches 200-205°F

4. **Cool**
   - Cool in pan for 10 minutes
   - Turn out onto wire rack
   - Cool completely before slicing (at least 1 hour)

## Streusel Topping (Optional)

### Ingredients

- 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar
- 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
- 2 tablespoons cold butter, cubed
- 1/4 cup chopped nuts (optional)

### Instructions

1. Combine flour, sugars, and cinnamon in small bowl
2. Cut in cold butter with fork or pastry cutter until crumbly
3. Stir in nuts if using
4. Sprinkle over batter before baking

## Tips for Success

### Banana Ripeness

- **Best:** Bananas with lots of brown spots, nearly black
- **Good:** Mostly yellow with brown spots
- **Avoid:** Yellow or green bananas (not sweet enough)
- **Hack:** Bake unpeeled bananas at 300°F for 15-20 minutes to ripen quickly

### Texture Tips

- Don't overmix the batter - stir until just combined
- Room temperature eggs blend better
- The yogurt/sour cream creates moisture and tenderness
- Letting the bread cool completely prevents gummy texture

### Storage

- **Room temperature:** Wrap tightly in plastic wrap, store up to 3 days
- **Refrigerator:** Wrap well, store up to 1 week (brings out flavors)
- **Freezer:** Wrap in plastic then foil, freeze up to 3 months
  - Thaw overnight in refrigerator
  - Or thaw at room temperature for 2-3 hours

### Variations

**Chocolate Banana Bread:**

- Add 1/4 cup cocoa powder to dry ingredients
- Increase sugar by 2 tablespoons
- Use chocolate chips

**Peanut Butter Banana Bread:**

- Replace 1/4 cup butter with peanut butter
- Swirl additional peanut butter on top before baking

**Vegan Version:**

- Replace eggs with flax eggs (2 tablespoons ground flax + 6 tablespoons water)
- Use coconut oil instead of butter
- Use coconut yogurt instead of Greek yogurt

**Gluten-Free:**

- Replace all-purpose flour with 1:1 gluten-free baking blend
- Add 1/4 teaspoon xanthan gum if blend doesn't include it

**Mini Loaves:**

- Divide batter among 3 mini loaf pans
- Bake at 350°F for 30-35 minutes

**Muffins:**

- Fill muffin tins 3/4 full
- Bake at 350°F for 18-22 minutes
- Makes about 12 muffins

## Troubleshooting

| Problem       | Cause                            | Solution                            |
| ------------- | -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Sunken middle | Underbaked or too much leavening | Bake longer, check oven temp        |
| Dry texture   | Overbaked or overmixed           | Check at 50 minutes, mix gently     |
| Dense/gummy   | Too much banana or underbaked    | Measure banana accurately           |
| Cracked top   | Oven too hot                     | Verify temperature with thermometer |
| Stuck to pan  | Insufficient greasing            | Use parchment paper liner           |

## Make Ahead

The batter can be prepared and refrigerated for up to 24 hours before baking. Bring to room temperature for 30 minutes before baking.

## Serving Suggestions

- Warm with a pat of butter
- Toasted with cream cheese
- With a smear of peanut butter
- Alongside morning coffee or tea
- As French toast (slice thick, dip in egg mixture, pan fry)
